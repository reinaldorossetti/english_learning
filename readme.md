## 🚀 Step-by-Step Study Plan

### 📌 Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–2): Build Your Verb Base

**Goal:** Internalize the 200 most common verbs with correct pronunciation and tenses.

**Step 1 — Open `200_verbs_english.MD`**

- Study **20 verbs per day** — one full section at a time
- Read each verb **aloud** using the phonetic guide
- Cover the table and try to recall the **past tense from memory**
- Write 1–2 sentences using the verb in an IT work context

**Step 2 — Apply Spaced Repetition**

- Review on days: **1 → 2 → 5 → 10 → 20**
- Create flashcards in Anki or Quizlet (front = verb, back = translation + past tense)
- Test yourself without looking at the table (active recall)

**Step 3 — Build a Daily Habit**

- Study **20–30 minutes every morning** before work — consistency is more powerful than long sessions
- Keep a notebook or digital doc to log verbs you confuse or forget

---

### 📌 Phase 2 — Technical English (Weeks 3–4): Learn IT-Specific Vocabulary

**Goal:** Expand vocabulary to cover your real daily work context.

**Step 1 — Switch to `200_verbs_it_english.MD`**

- Prioritize sections matching your current role:
  - QA? Focus on Testing & QA (41–60) and Version Control (81–100)
  - DevOps? Focus on DevOps/CI-CD (61–80) and Cloud (161–180)
  - Backend Dev? Focus on Programming (21–40) and Database (101–120)
- For each verb, recall a **real situation from your last project** and form a sentence

**Step 2 — Practice Career Verbs**

- Open `interview_verbs_career.MD` and study 10 verbs per day
- For each verb, complete this template:

  > *"I [verb] the [thing] by [method], which resulted in [outcome]."*
  > Example: *"I **reduced** test flakiness by 60% by implementing explicit waits across the suite."*

**Step 3 — Write in English at Work**

- Write all **commit messages** in English
- Write **Jira/YouTrack tickets** and bug reports in English
- Write **Slack messages** to teammates in English when possible

---

### 📌 Phase 3 — Interview Preparation (Weeks 5–6): Simulate Real Interviews

**Goal:** Be able to answer any technical interview question fluently and confidently in English.

**Step 1 — Study Manual Testing Interview File**

- Open `interview_qa_manual_testing.MD`
- Pick **3 questions per day**, write your own answer, then say it aloud without looking
- Record yourself on your phone and listen back — identify weak vocabulary or hesitations

**Step 2 — Study Automation & DevOps Interview File**

- Open `interview_qa_automation_devops.MD`
- Focus on sections that match your stack (Playwright, Appium, CI/CD, etc.)
- Personalize the model answers with your **real project details and numbers**

**Step 3 — Full Dialogue Simulation**

- Open `tips_english_interview_qa.MD`
- Read both the **interviewer and candidate roles aloud** in a single session
- Ask a colleague to play the interviewer and conduct a timed mock interview in English

---

### 📌 Phase 4 — Integration & Fluency (Weeks 7–8): Connect It All

**Goal:** Actively use English every day across multiple real channels.

**Step 1 — Write Daily**

- Write a **3-sentence daily work log in English**: what you worked on, what you achieved, what blocked you
- Keep updating `CURRICULUM_VITAE_2025_EN.MD` as you deliver new features or complete certifications

**Step 2 — Watch + Listen Daily**

- Watch **1 YouTube video per day** from the [channels listed below](#-youtube-channels-for-english-learning)
- Listen to **1 tech podcast episode per week** (Syntax.fm, Software Engineering Daily, The Changelog)

**Step 3 — Use English in Real Tools**

- Switch your IDE language to English if it isn't already
- Read documentation in English **without translating** (Playwright, Appium, Robot Framework)
- Use Grammarly or DeepL when writing emails in English — study the suggestions

---

### 📌 Phase 5 — Maintenance (Ongoing): Keep It Alive

**Goal:** Sustain and deepen your skills indefinitely.

- ✅ Review **10 verbs per week** from any file in this repo
- ✅ Watch **3–4 YouTube videos per week** from the channels below
- ✅ Use English in **at least one work context per day** (commits, messages, tickets)
- ✅ Conduct a **full mock interview in English once a month**
- ✅ Read one **English tech article per day** — Dev.to, Medium, InfoQ, or Martin Fowler's blog

## 💡 Pro Tips for IT Professionals

### 🔑 High-Impact Daily Habits

- **Write all commit messages in English** — even simple ones like `fix: handle null token in auth middleware` train your brain naturally
- **Write Jira/YouTrack tickets in English** — describe the bug, steps to reproduce, expected vs. actual behavior
- **Use Grammarly or DeepL** when writing emails or Slack messages in English — always review the suggestions to learn from them
- **Read the official documentation** of your tools directly in English — Playwright, Appium, Robot Framework, Docker — stop translating
- **Think directly in English** — when forming a question or statement, try to produce it in English before defaulting to translation

### 🗣️ Speaking Improvement Tricks

- **Record yourself** answering an interview question and listen back — identify where you hesitate or lose vocabulary
- **Shadow native speakers** — pause a YouTube video and repeat the exact sentence with the same rhythm and intonation (repeat 3 times)
- **Use the STAR method** for every behavioral answer: **S**ituation → **T**ask → **A**ction → **R**esult
- **Don't wait until you're "ready"** — imperfect spoken English practiced daily beats perfect English never spoken

### 📝 Writing Practice Tricks

- **Write a 3-sentence daily work log in English** — what you worked on, what you delivered, what you'll do tomorrow
- **Translate your full LinkedIn profile to English** and review it monthly — add new achievements using verbs from `interview_verbs_career.MD`
- **Write PR descriptions in English** — describe the change, why it was made, and how to test it

### 🎧 Listening & Immersion Tricks

- **Enable English subtitles** on everything you watch — even when watching in Portuguese, add an English subtitle track
- **Listen to a tech podcast for 15 minutes daily** while commuting or exercising — Syntax.fm, The Changelog, or Lex Fridman Podcast
- **Follow English-speaking tech professionals** on LinkedIn and read every post carefully
